I am an Assistant Professor in the Faculty of Library, Information and Media Science at the University of Tsukuba, Japan. My research interests are in human–computer interaction, aging and accessibility and user interface optimization.

Prior to joining Tsukuba, I worked as a Research Associate in the School of Information at Kochi University of Technology, Japan, where I was a member of Center for Human-Engaged Computing directed by Prof. Xiangshi Ren. Before that, I obtained PhD degree from IIT Kharagpur, master and bachelor degrees from West Bengal University of Technology, India.

My research sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Accessibility. In particular, my research emphasizes on improving human individual abilities through developing intelligent systems using design and modelling practices, specifically focused on individual differences in users’ sensorimotor abilities toward technology adoption.
