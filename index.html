I am an Assistant Professor in the Graduate School of Library, Information and Media Studies at the University of Tsukuba, Japan. My research interests are in human–computer interaction, ageing and accessibility and user interface optimization.

Prior to joining Tsukuba, I worked as a Research Associate in the School of Information at Kochi University of Technology, Japan, where I was a member of Center for Human-Engaged Computing directed by Prof. Xiangshi Ren. Before that, I obtained PhD degree from IIT Kharagpur, master and bachelor degrees from West Bengal University of Technology, India.

My research sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Accessibility. In particular, my research emphasizes on improving human individual abilities through developing intelligent systems using design and modelling practices, specifically focused on individual differences in usersâ€™ sensorimotor abilities toward technology adoption.

Working with me

    I am always looking for hard-working, self-motivated, and scientifically curious students to join my research group. A range of graduate and undergraduate projects are available. The candidate must have strong skills in computer programming, interaction design, and/or quantitative research methods. Visit Information for Prospective Students for further details. Current University of Tsukuba students can contact directly over email to express interest in working with me. A few JSPS Postdoctoral Fellow positions and unpaid on-campus or remote volunteering opportunities are also available.
